Transactions, Business Formation and Governance

A great deal of litigation can be prevented if proper steps are taken on the front end regarding entity formation, commercial transactions and corporate governance. You can get a favorable outcome if you take care to safeguard your rights at the time your company was formed or the contract was written. The business law services we offer start at the time of company formation, and continue throughout the lifetime of the company. Count on the entity formation lawyers at MichieHamlett to help you understand the pros and cons of establishing your business as a corporation, partnership or hybrid entity such as an LLC or S corp. We'll help you set up your company as a registered legal entity licensed to do business in the state and make sure you start off with all the company documents, policies and agreements you need to get started.

As your business gets underway, our business transaction attorneys are here to negotiate, draft or review any contract or agreement, including employment contracts and independent contractor agreements, vendor and supplier contracts, asset purchases, lending and more. MichieHamlet can be a trusted resource for assistance with corporate governance matters, including drafting, interpreting, and reviewing buy-sell and vendor agreements, as well understanding correct voting procedures and compliance with securities regulations.

The Secretary of State

The Secretary of State can provide information on starting a business within your state. Most states have websites that offer detailed information about how to form and register a business in their area.

Some states also offer online legal resources such as templates for articles of incorporation or LLC formation documents, which you can use to get started. The National Association of Secretaries of State website allows you to search for and obtain contact information of the Secretary of State in your area.

Small businesses should also be aware of their state's laws and regulations, as well as any licenses or permits that may be required to operate in that area. This information can be found on the website of your state for the Secretary or you may contact the Small Business Development Center for help.

The Chamber Of Commerce in your area

Your local chamber of commerce is another place you could get legal advice for free. Many chambers offer members access to lawyers who can answer questions about specific legal issues affecting small businesses in their area, as well as other benefits.

You will need to join the chamber, just like your small business association. However, there are some chambers that offer free membership for entrepreneurs who are just starting out. You can find your local chamber of commerce by visiting the website of an organization like the United States Chamber of Commerce or contacting the nearest Small Business Development Center (SBDC) for help.
